Create, Manage, and Share Beautiful Media Slideshows with Ease.

Welcome to LightBox!

LightBox is a user-friendly web application designed to make the creation and sharing of media slideshows simple and elegant. Whether you're a hobbyist looking to share memories, a student working on a project, or a developer interested in a full-stack Flask application, LightBox offers a range of features built on a modern tech stack.

Why Try LightBox?

  • Simplicity: Intuitive interface for uploading, organizing, and playing media.
  • Versatility: Handles images, videos (with MKV conversion), and audio.
  • Performance: Background processing for tasks like video conversion ensures a responsive user experience.
  • Learn & Explore: An excellent educational example of a Flask application using Celery for task queues, Redis for data storage, and FFmpeg for media manipulation.
  • Open Source: Free to use, modify, and learn from. Contributions are welcome!

Getting Started is Easy:

  1. 1. Upload Your Media:

    Select your images (JPG, PNG, etc.), videos (MP4, MOV, WebM), or audio files (MP3, WAV). You can create a new "Lightbox" (our term for a collection) or add files to an existing one.

  2. 2. View Your Gallery:

    Once uploaded, your media items appear in a gallery view. Here you can manage them, see their status (especially for MKVs being processed), hide/unhide, or like items.

  3. 3. Launch the Slideshow:

    Click the prominent "Play" button on a Lightbox to instantly view your compatible media in a smooth, full-featured presentation with easy-to-use playback controls, fullscreen mode, and keyboard/touch navigation.

  4. 4. Manage & Share:

    Rename your Lightboxes, add more files, export your collection, or delete them. You can also generate public share links to show your Lightboxes to others.

A Note on MKV File Uploads:

LightBox readily accepts MKV files! To ensure smooth and universal playback across all browsers and devices, when you upload an MKV file, our system automatically converts it in the background to a highly compatible MP4 format. This process uses industry-standard H.264 video and AAC audio codecs, which are ideal for web streaming.

You will see a "Processing..." status for these files in your gallery. They will become available for slideshows once this conversion is complete. The time taken depends on the size and complexity of your MKV file.

For those interested in the technical details, the typical FFmpeg command used for this conversion is similar to:

ffmpeg -i [input_mkv_file] \
    -c:v libx264 \
    -preset fast \
    -crf 23 \
    -c:a aac \
    -b:a 128k \
    -movflags +faststart \
    -y [output_mp4_file]

Future Enhancements: We are exploring options for user-selectable quality presets for MKV conversion (e.g., higher quality but slower processing) and potentially allowing advanced users to customize some FFmpeg parameters.

Open Source, Learning & Contribution

LightBox is proudly an open-source project, developed with a passion for learning and sharing. It serves as an excellent educational example of a modern web application, showcasing technologies like Flask, Celery for background tasks, Redis as a NoSQL database, and FFmpeg for media processing, all wrapped in a user-friendly interface built with Bootstrap and JavaScript.
